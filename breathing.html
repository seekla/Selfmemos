Breathing and stress

When we're stressed we take small and shallow inhales that stop in our chest before exhaling.[1] We can reduce stress in our bodies by intentionally changing the way we breathe. One way to destress is to breathe deep into your abdomen.[2]




Belly (abdominal) breathing:

  • 1) Sit in a comfortable position with one hand on your chest and the other on your belly
  • 2) Slowly breathe in (inhale) through your nose
  • 3) Let the breath move into your chest and down into your belly
  • 4) The breath should fill up your belly, allowing it to expand and push your hand out
  • 5) Slowly breathe out (exhale) from your mouth or nose (whichever is most comfortable for you)
